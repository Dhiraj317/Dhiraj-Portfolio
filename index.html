Design and Analysis of Liquid Infused Surfaces for Water-Oil Separation
capillary imbibition selective adsorption capillary diode water-oil separation

A capillary device is designed and fabricated in glass to work as a fluidic diode with vanishingly small hydrodynamic conductance for imbibition of water within a finite range of immersion depths. This is attained through patterning a section of predefined length on the device surfaces using a single-step laserbased ablation process and without resorting to chemical treatment of the hydrophilic glass substrate. While the studied device works as a fluidic diode for water, it can behave as a conventional capillary slit for the imbibition of oils (e.g., alkanes, silicone oils) with low surface tension. A prototype device with simple geometric design (see Fig.) is demonstrated for selective adsorption and separation of water and oil in vertical imbibition experiments at controlled immersion depths. Efficient devices for passive separation of water and oil can be designed based on the demonstrated physical mechanism and the analytical model proposed in this research project.

Fluid Flow characterization using X-ray Spectroscopy techniques
Python Jupyter Notebook Optimization Algorithms

X-ray photon correlation spectroscopy (XPCS), a novel x-ray scattering technique analogous to dynamic light scattering, enables probing the hydrodynamics of liquids on length scales typically ranging from nanometers to microns and time scales ranging from microseconds to minutes. My research at the Coherent Hard X-ray Scattering (CHX) beamline of the National Synchrotron Light Source (NSLS-II) where the XPCS techniques are employed to characterize flow velocity profiles and mass diffusivity of complex colloidal fluids under continuous flow.

Built a series of data pipelines for analyzing datasets in Jupyter Notebook. Specifically, a flow decomposition technique is developed to study flow profiles in laminar regime. The techniques and analytical methods developed here can provide new insights into the hydrodynamic and rheological behavior of liquids in micro and nanoscale confinement.

Dynamic contact angle experiments of solids with plain & textured coatings
MATLAB

Surface Tension plays a critical role in the movement of insects on water, formation of meniscus inside capillaries, emulsions, capillary bridges. Moreover, the wetting behavior of liquids is governed by it's interaction with solid substrates. Specifically, contact angle plays a phenomenon role in the ability of surfaces to wet and de-wet. Dynamic contact angle analysis is performed using a Force Tensiometer to characterize the wettability of surfaces with plain & nano textures on borosilicate glass.
